Unveiling the Dark Side: How Nation-State Actors Drive Cybercrime Revenue in North Korea

Introduction

Did you know that Cybercrime ranks among the top 5 revenue sources for North Korea? As reported by CNBC in 2017, the government's hyper-focus on weapon development, exacerbated by economic sanctions, has led them to leverage the cyber realm to generate income.

The Dictatorial Landscape

Much like China and Russia, North Korea operates under a dictatorship where compliance is non-negotiable. The consequences of non-compliance can be severe. Economic sanctions have forced North Korea to intensify its efforts in weapon development and cyber operations as alternative revenue streams.

The Global Hacker Presence

North Korea boasts a significant hacker presence worldwide. While it's hard to confirm whether they are the absolute best, what is clear is that North Korea employs a vast number of citizens to engage in hacking activities. This has become a de facto "legal" government job for many unfortunate North Korean citizens.

Exploring Threat Actors

IBM outlines several types of threat actors, including Cybercriminals, Nation-state actors, Hacktivists, Thrill seekers, Insider threats, and Cyberterrorists. In the context of this article, the primary focus is on Nation-state actors.

Conclusion

The cyber landscape is fraught with diverse threat actors, each with its own motivations and methods. However, the spotlight in this piece is on Nation-state actors, particularly examining North Korea's unique circumstances and their utilization of cyber operations as a means of economic sustenance.
